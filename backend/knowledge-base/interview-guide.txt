Interview Preparation Guide — Knowledge Base Document
=====================================================

STAR Method for Behavioral Interviews
--------------------------------------
The STAR method is a structured approach to answering behavioral interview questions:

S — Situation: Describe the context and background. Set the scene with relevant details.
T — Task: Explain what you were responsible for. What was your role?
A — Action: Detail the specific steps you took. Focus on YOUR contributions.
R — Result: Share the outcome. Use metrics and data when possible.

Common Behavioral Questions:
- Tell me about a time you faced a challenging deadline
- Describe a situation where you had to work with a difficult team member
- Give an example of when you showed leadership
- Tell me about a time you failed and what you learned
- Describe a situation where you had to make a difficult decision

Technical Interview Best Practices
-----------------------------------
1. Think out loud — Interviewers want to see your thought process
2. Ask clarifying questions before diving into a solution
3. Start with a brute force approach, then optimize
4. Consider edge cases and error handling
5. Discuss time and space complexity
6. Write clean, readable code with meaningful variable names
7. Test your solution with examples

Common Technical Topics:
- Data Structures: Arrays, Linked Lists, Trees, Graphs, Hash Maps
- Algorithms: Sorting, Searching, Dynamic Programming, Recursion
- System Design: Scalability, Load Balancing, Caching, Database Sharding
- Web Technologies: REST APIs, GraphQL, Authentication, WebSockets
- DevOps: CI/CD, Containers, Cloud Services, Monitoring

System Design Interview Framework
-----------------------------------
1. Requirements Clarification (5 min)
   - Functional requirements
   - Non-functional requirements (scale, latency, availability)
   - Constraints and assumptions

2. High-Level Design (10 min)
   - API design
   - Data model
   - Component architecture diagram

3. Detailed Design (15 min)
   - Deep dive into critical components
   - Database schema
   - Caching strategy
   - Message queues and async processing

4. Scaling and Trade-offs (10 min)
   - Horizontal vs vertical scaling
   - Database sharding
   - CDN and edge computing
   - Monitoring and alerting

Resume Best Practices
---------------------
- Use action verbs: Developed, Implemented, Led, Optimized, Reduced, Increased
- Quantify achievements: "Reduced load time by 40%", "Led team of 5 engineers"
- Keep it to 1-2 pages
- Use consistent formatting
- Include relevant keywords for ATS systems
- Highlight projects with measurable impact
- Include both technical and soft skills
- Tailor your resume to each application

Communication Skills in Interviews
------------------------------------
- Listen actively — don't interrupt
- Structure your answers clearly
- Use the "headline first" approach — state your main point, then elaborate
- Ask thoughtful questions at the end
- Show enthusiasm for the role and company
- Be honest about what you don't know
- Follow up with a thank-you email

Common HR Interview Questions
-------------------------------
- Why are you interested in this role?
- What are your greatest strengths and weaknesses?
- Where do you see yourself in 5 years?
- Why are you leaving your current position?
- What is your expected salary range?
- Describe your ideal work environment
- How do you handle stress and pressure?

Salary Negotiation Tips
------------------------
1. Research market rates using Glassdoor, Levels.fyi, and Payscale
2. Consider the total compensation package (base, bonus, equity, benefits)
3. Never give the first number — let the company make an offer
4. Express enthusiasm but ask for time to consider
5. Counter with data and rationale
6. Be prepared to walk away if the offer doesn't meet your minimum

Post-Interview Follow-Up
-------------------------
- Send a thank-you email within 24 hours
- Reference specific conversation points
- Reiterate your interest and qualifications
- Keep it brief and professional
- Follow up again after one week if you haven't heard back
